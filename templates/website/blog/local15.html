Why Local Cabs Are Key to Better Public Transport

1. First-Mile and Last-Mile Connectivity:
Local cabs provide a crucial link between public transport hubs and passengers' final destinations, filling gaps where buses or trains may not reach.

2. Flexibility in Transport Options:
Unlike fixed-route public transport, local cabs offer on-demand, door-to-door service, allowing for greater flexibility and convenience, especially for those with limited mobility.

3. Reducing Congestion:
By integrating local cabs with public transport systems, cities can reduce traffic congestion as more people opt for combined public and private transport solutions.

4. Supporting Nighttime and Off-Hours Transport:
Local cabs are often available during late hours when public transport is less frequent, providing essential services to people working or traveling at odd hours.

5. Integration with Smart Transport Systems:
Many cities are integrating local cabs into their smart transport grids, allowing passengers to switch seamlessly between public transport and taxi services through unified apps and payment systems.
